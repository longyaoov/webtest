The Issue: Churches risk criminal charges to offer 'sanctuary' to refugees

Sunday, February 7, 2016 
by John Cleary

| AAP


Related Audio:

The Issue: Churches risk criminal charges to offer 'sanctuary' to refugees


Download as MP3

Sanctuary: it is an ancient word usually associated with the Middle Ages, and the offer of churches as places of protection for those fearing physical attack and violence. This week churches in Australia, have offered Sanctuary to refugees at risk of being sent back to Nauru, following a High Court decision early in the week. The hope is to pressure the government into a change of heart.

Earlier today on ABC TV's Insiders, Prime Minister Malcolm Turnbull declared that the line must be held, in order to dissuade people smugglers from recommencing their evil trade. But if anything the call for Sanctuary is widening. Two of our largest Catholic healthcare organisations, St Vincents and Calvary, have now offered to provide medical support to any of the 267 asylum seekers applying for church sanctuary to avoid their forced removal to Nauru. They are encouraging other hospital groups to do the same.

GUESTS IN THIS HOUR

    Misha Coleman, Executive Officer of the Churches Refugee Task Force

    Rev. Peter Catt, Anglican Dean of Brisbane, who has offered his Cathedral as a place of Sanctuary

    Rev. Elenie Poulos, National Director of Uniting Justice, the justice arm of the Uniting Church in Australia

    Rev. Chris Bedding, rector of the Anglican Church at Darlington in Perth, a local congregation that has declared themselves open to offering Sanctuary
