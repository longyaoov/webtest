Why Courchevel Is The Ultimate Ski Destination

It has everything to do with the food, drink, and aprés-ski scene

By Hans AschimPublished: 08 November 2018

European ski destinations often feature a rich network of villages connected by a menagerie of chairlifts, gondolas, roads, and Wes Anderson-esque funiculars. No destination embodies the charm, luxury, and awe-inspiring vistas quite like France’s Courchevel.

Located high in the Alps in the southeast part of the country, Courchevel is part of Les Trois Vallées, the largest ski area in the world that sees eight different resorts connected by lifts to create nearly 400 miles of skiable runs.

Best of all, many of the slopes in Courchevel are north-facing, meaning it’s likely you’ll find the best snow conditions in the Alps. And, once you're done skiing for the day—or if you choose to take a mid-day break—Courchevel has an energetic food and drink scene. Here, a look at where to eat, drink, après-ski and indulge in all Courchevel has to offer, plus enticing styles from Moncler Grenoble to make it all look stylish. A collection of pieces suited for the slopes and beyond, the label—named for the French village where it originated in 1952—has gone back to its roots by mixing traditional fabrics with technical features.

En Piste: The Best On-Mountain Eateries

There are dozens of restaurants nestled among the peaks in Courchevel. You'll find cozy refuges serving rustic Savoyard fare, sprawling sun-decks where the party begins as soon as the chairlifts start running, and Michelin-starred dining rooms that rival those in Paris.

The best way to enjoy a mountain lunch in the Alps is by first stimulating your appetite with some skiing. Check the "Piste du Jour" while enjoying your morning coffee and croissant to find the best conditions of the day. If you’re planning on going off-piste—that’s out of bounds of the ski resort—to chase powder, make sure you have the necessary avalanche safety gear and training, or hire a guide for the day.

On a clear, sunny day, make your first turns on the Combe de la Saulire. Widely considered one of the best runs in the world, it's nearly two miles of wide, steep north-facing pitch with panoramic views begging for high speed turns.

After a few laps on this bucket-list Alps descent, head to La Cave des Creuxs. Enjoy the sundeck and outdoor fire pits where traditional regional Savoyard comfort food is the order of the day. Opt for the lamb cooked on the fireplace and finish your meal off the local way with an espresso and a nip of génépy, a liqueur made with mountain herbs.

Image: 3 Moncler Grenoble Telluride Jacket; Turtleneck Sweater; Salopette; all available at moncler.com

Image: 3 Moncler Grenoble Gelt Jacket; Telluride Jacket; Turtleneck Sweater; Salopette; all available at moncler.com

If the snow is falling and the visibility is poor, set your sights on a lower elevation. La Tania is the go-to spot for skiing in the trees, and it's where you’ll find most locals on a powder day.

Whether it’s snowy or the clouds have overtaken the mountains, head to La Soucoupe at the top of the Col de la Loze chairlift for a decadent lunch. When conditions aren't ideal for skiing, they're ideal for dining. The cosy, rustic atmosphere lends itself well to lingering lunches over wine. Make a point to try the house-made diots, a sausage particular to the region. Lunches here tend to drift late into the afternoon, so save this meal for after you’ve had your fill of skiing for the day.

Aprés Ski: A Way of Life in the Alps

Whether it’s the best ski day of the season or bitterly cold and icy, there’s always one constant in Courchevel—after the lifts stop running, the real party starts. Aprés-ski in the French resort town is one of the main draws, and across the five villages there’s everything from hole-in-the-wall dives to some of the most luxurious clubs in Europe that are all worth checking out.

Image (above—on men): 3 Moncler Grenoble Turtleneck Sweater; Salopette; all available at moncler.com

If you’re looking to hang with the locals and party with your ski gear on, head to La Boulotte in Courchevel 1650. Expect homemade food and a colourful crowd in this centrally located pub; start out with a glass of vin chaud to warm up.

Should live music be an essential part of your aprés checklist, visit Le Catérail in the heart of Courchevel 1550. This hotspot has the look and feel of a rustic mountain refuge, but is home to a rotating cast of international DJs and bands. The party turns up the most on Thursdays, so plan accordingly.

For the full-on night club experience, look no further than La Grange in Courchevel 1850. This plush club has a Moroccan vibe with cosy sofas, bottle service, and nightly high profile DJs. Stop by after changing out of your ski gear, as this spot doesn’t really heat up until around 11 p.m. And maybe don’t plan on catching first chair the next day.

Image (above): 3 Moncler Grenoble Telluride Jacket; Baseball Hat; all available at moncler.com

The True Raison d’Être: Dining in Courchevel

It’s no question that the dramatic mountain views and world class skiing are what draw most visitors to Courchevel, but after a few meals it’s easy to see why many plan their days around their dinner reservations. The high alpine network of villages is home to some of the top chefs in France, and plays host to a long, rich culinary history.

Explore local cuisine in rustic but comfortable surroundings at Le Restaurant Pilatus that overlooks Courchevel 1850’s exclusive airstrip. Order the classic tartiflette, a Savoyard dish of potatoes, reblochon cheese, lardons, and onions.

Image (right—on him): 3 Moncler Grenoble Telluride Jacket; Salopette; Baseball hat; all available at moncler.com

The Savoyard region is known for its rich foods and longtime use of truffle mushrooms in special dishes. There’s no better place to indulge in this tradition that La Saulire, with its cosy-but-chic cabin vibes and panoramic views. The celebrated restaurant specialises in truffle Savoyard dishes, so don’t hesitate when ordering the truffle fondue. Ask your server for the best wine pairing, as the availability of nearby vintages is always changing.

Finding a memorable meal in Courchevel isn’t difficult, but for one that will truly change the way you experience food there’s the two-Michelin starred Le Chabichou. Located in the fairytale-esque hotel of the same name, dining at Le Chabichou is an all-evening affair and best enjoyed with the chef’s tasting menu. Using the best ingredients along with both classical and innovative techniques, Chefs Michel Rochedy and Stéphane Buron have earned numerous international accolades over the years making this one of the most sought after spots in the Alps.
