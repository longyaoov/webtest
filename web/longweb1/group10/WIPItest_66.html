    NEW DELHI: What a week it was!

    The domestic market broke below key support levels, but still managed to crawl back and end the week in the green. The Nifty50, which had slipped below the 7,950 mark, managed to close with marginal gains of 0.5 per cent for the week ended November 25.

    The S&P BSE Sensex ended the week 0.6 per cent higher.

    The market is likely to remain rangebound in the coming week, as traders would like to stay on the sidelines ahead of the US Federal Reserve’s policy review due next month.

    The domestic market finally ended its four-week-long losing streak and managed to close on a flat note. Relentless selling by foreign institutional investors (FIIs) in domestic market added to the worries of market participants and pushed the rupee to a record low against the dollar.

      The rupee managed to regain ground after hitting a record low last seen in August 2013. The domestic currency ended the week at 68.47 to the dollar, up 0.4 per cent, after notching up its biggest gain since September 22 compared with its previous close of 68.57.

      Some analysts ETMarkets.com spoke to last week said the local currency was on track to hit the 70 mark in the near term. The rupee depreciated more than 3.5 per cent over the past 10 days.

      “The impact of demonetisation of high-value currency notes and hints of a December rate hike by the US Federal Reserve continued to impact equity market and the rupee,” said Abnish Kumar Sudhanshu, Director & Research Head, Amrapali Aadya Trading & Investments.

      “Over the past 10 sessions following the demonetisation move, FIIs have sold approximately Rs 13,570 crore worth of stocks. The bond market has also suffered badly and has seen outflow of approximately Rs 24,000 crore of FII money in the timeframe,” he said.

      Going by the buzz on Dalal Street, here is a list of five factors that could chart market direction during the coming week.

      GDP print for September quarter: Following demonetisation, investors are likely to remain cautious ahead of the September quarter GDP (gross domestic product) data to be released on November 30.

      Former prime minister Manmohan Singh on Thursday said the move caused a lot of hardship to the common man and GDP growth is likely to see a 2 per cent cut in FY17.

      The GDP of Asia’s second-largest economy expanded 7.1 per cent in three months till June end against a 7.9 per cent acceleration recorded at the start of the year, the Central Statistics Office said.

      “Q3 GDP growth data is scheduled to be released next week, which investors will be watching closely followed by the Nikkei manufacturing PMI for November. The two data sets are likely to have a major impact on market trend next week,” said Rohit Gadia, Founder & CEO, CapitalVia Global Research.

      Other macroeconomic data: Apart from GDP data, investors will keenly watch infrastructure output data due on November 30 and Nikkei Manufacturing PMI data for November due on Thursday, December 1.

      India’s manufacturing picked up to a 22-month high in October. Nikkei India Manufacturing Purchasing Managers Index (PMI) climbed to 54.4 in October from 52.1 in the previous month.

      “Market participants will be keenly eyeing some of important macroeconomic data such as GDP print, core sector data and manufacturing PMI over the next week,” Jayant Manglik, President, Retail Distribution, Religare Securities, told ETMarkets.com.

      Nov auto sales numbers: Four-wheeler and two-wheeler stocks will be in focus in the coming week, as November sales data will be announced starting December 1. Stocks like Maruti Suzuki, Hero MotoCorp, Bajaj Auto and Ashok Leyland will be on the watch list.

      Opec meet: US crude prices fell nearly 4 per cent on the last trading day of the week, dragged by uncertainty over whether the Organisation of the Petroleum Exporting Countries (Opec) will reach a deal on output freeze on November 30, after Saudi Arabia said it would not attend the talks with non-Opec producers on Monday.

      Brent crude futures settled at $47.24 a barrel. Prices continued to decline in post-settlement trading, dropping as low as $45.88 a barrel.

      “The scheduled Opec meeting may not have a sustainable impact on crude oil prices. Even if Opec decides to cut production, it will have no meaning, because the members do not follow any quota discipline, and Iraq and Iran will not attend the meeting and continue with heightened supplies,” Amar Ambani, IIFL Private Wealth, said.

      “Besides, an attack on a Nigerian pipeline has already halted some production. So a further output cut is not certain. Even if prices rise, they are unlikely to sustain,” he said.

      Technical outlook: The Nifty50 closed the week on a positive note despite a lot of volatility. But followup buying is necessary if the bulls want to make a comeback on Dalal Street, experts said.

      After a sharp fall, most of the momentum indicators have reached the oversold zone, indicating that a pullback is on the cards. However, an oversold market does not always ensure a pullback, and price action is required to confirm the same.

      “Until a pullback is observed, we will continue our negative outlook for the medium term and want to see how the market behaves next week around the key supply level of 8,200,” Rohit Gadia, Founder & CEO, CapitalVia Global Research, told ETMarkets.com.

      “Sustaining above this level will change the market outlook to bullish and a move above the 8,400 mark will further improve the outlook. Any break below the 7,950 level will raise the chances of a resumption of the downtrend,” he said.
