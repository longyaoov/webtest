    New Delhi: Congress treasurer Ahmed Patel has accused the Centre of misusing government agencies to target opposition parties. The attack came after I-T raids at party’s chief accountant SM Moin’s residence in the Capital on Monday.

    “This is sheer frustration. They are misusing government agencies and trying to create a situation where opposition isn’t in a position to fight elections. But elections aren’t fought with money. They will come to know about that after the polls are over.”

    Patel also accused the government of “tapping the phones of opposition leaders.” “But we are not scared.”

    Patel’s name cropped up in the controversy after a picture of him at Moin’s house appeared in the media. “If my chief accountant has not come to office for entire day and his family members have no clue about him, it is my responsibility to go and find about his wellbeing.

    I went there yesterday and no one stopped me or said anything,” he said, adding he was there for 10 minutes when a constable and an officer came and requested for a photo. “I said yes.”
