 The Game.Ars Report
by Carl Salminen

 

It's almost time. Yep, it's just a few short days before E3 starts up. Or perhaps it's tomorrow depending on when you read this. Anyway, what really matters is that E3 is gonna give every gamer what he/she/it wants. Something that I have come to call The Big Tease! No, I'm not talking about booth babes.

Promises will flow like your colon after a fried-bean festival. And as it is with the politicians who perfected them, these promises are rarely kept. Or maybe it's just poor communication. Nah, it's all lies and marketing banter.

So, to help you understand the dialects known as Developer Speak and Producer Spew, I have created this handy translator that will help you discern just what is being said at the upcoming E3 conference.

They say this:

They mean this:
“It is due later this year.” “Don't hold your breath, sucker.”
“It's still being tweaked.”  "Half of our team walked out on us mid-way through the development cycle.”
“It'll turn first-person shooters on their ear!” “It's a Quake clone.”
“Meet Lana, our booth babe.” “Please don't look, the demo just crashed our rig.”
“Here, have a complimentary key chain.” “Please Mr. Publisher, pick up our game!”
“Here, this is Lana's room key." “Please Mr. Reviewer, don't trash our game!”
"It'll run on a 200 MHz Pentium."  "It choked the computers at NASA."

Okay, you're ready for all the spew that Mount Saint E3 can dump on you.

And stay tuned for next weekend's E3 Special Edition of Game.Ars. Ideas? Suggestions? Send 'em here.

5/7/2000 Edition
 

MS Flight Simulator pilots can visit the Canyon

If you're a virtual pilot and Microsoft Flight Simulator 2000 player, then take heed, because earlier this week Wilco Publishing released a patch for its Grand Canyon scenery pack that adds compatibility for Microsoft's Flight Simulator.

The scenery includes a combined 12,000 square miles of scenery. For a visit to the Grand Canyon go here. No pack mules required.

Electronic Arts is getting branded

EA announced last week that they were going to consolidate their subsidiaries under one brand. Actually, 3 EA sub-brands: EA Games, EA.Com and EA Sports. Maxis, Westwood, Origin and Bullfrog will all loose their identities to the EA moniker.

No word has been mentioned about whether or not any prime properties would be axed or altered. I can't imagine that such recognizable products as Command & Conquer or Ultima Online would have anything to fear.

Honda and Microsoft to create a Pentium III powered motorcycle

Well, not really. What I meant to say is: Microsoft and Honda announced this week that they will work together to feature some Honda 2000 series off-road bikes in Motocross Madness 2 for the PC. Anyone who played the first Motocross Madness knows that we are in for a treat. It's the best off-road bike sim to date.

If you know your Hondas, then you can look forward to taking out one of these hot models: The CR 125R, CR 250R and the high-performance XR 400. Other models have yet to be announced. Please wear a helmet, it's the law.

Curious? Go to the official homepage at take a gander. Motocross Madness 2 is scheduled to be releases this month.

Metal Gear Solid to get a PC port

Rumor has it that a port for Konami's Metal Gear Solid is coming to the PC. Console gamers already know how great this game is and now the PC community will get its grubby mits on it. Let's hope they do a better job with this game than Squaresoft did with their Final Fantasy VIII port. Ugh!

Microsoft apparently told Gamespot.com that they would be showing Metal Gear Solid for the PC in playable form at E3. Can you hear me wringing my hands over this one?

Take 2 takes on G.O.D.

It appears that Gathering of Developers has been purchased by Take 2 interactive. Ironic that G.O.D., the developer-driven (read: anti-publisher) gang should be picked up by such a major, um, publisher. Most press releases state that G.O.D. will remain autonomous and continue developing their current line up of games. This includes titles like Heavy Metal F.A.K.K. 2, KISS Psycho Circus, Max Payne, Rune, 4 x 4 Evolution, and the Blair Witch games.

It should be interesting to see how things develop.

Opposing Force to get free CTF

Gearbox Software announced that they will release a Capture the Flag upgrade for Half-Life: Opposing Force. Opposing Force, the official expansion for Half-Life did not ship with CTF built in. Gearbox has taken it on and will release the upgrade for free sometime this month. This will be part an upcoming Opposing Force update.

Look for new weapons and maps once you've got it installed. Now that's what I'm talkin' 'bout. Free stuff. I'll let you know when it's available.

Fastest gaming machine in the entire universe

Enhanced Memory Systems is doing some horn tootin'. They claim that they are going to show off the world's fastest gaming rig at this year's E3. If you're wondering what they're packin', take a peek: 

  • 900 MHz Athlon (overclocked to 968 MHz)
  • ASUSTek K7-V motherboard
  • DDR GeForce 256 video
  • Their own, Low latency PC133 high speed DRAM overclocked to 144 MHz.

I guess we'll have to wait and see... Results will be posted after E3. I'll be watching. No, I'll probably be drooling. Still, it hardly sounds like the fastest gaming rig in town.  In fact, I think Caesar has a 1GHz Athlon with an overclocked GeForce2 GTS right this moment.

Ten classic games get Shocked

Midway Games and Shockwave have announced that ten of Midway's all-time classic arcade games are now available for your playing pleasure at shockwave.com. The ten titles include Defender, Joust, Spy Hunter, Rampage, Robotron 2084, Defender II, Sinistar, Bubbles, Tapper and Satan's Hollow. 

Oh boy, these were the games that I breast-fed my gaming habit on. Get over there - after you read Game.Ars that is.

I'd buy that for a dollar!

The following list details a few games that I have recently seen in software retailers selling for under $10. Yep, these titles, all AAA releases, can be found for $9.99 or less. Cheap prices, not cheap games. I've included links to their official homepages, respectively.

Total Annihilation: Kingdoms - Cavedog
Heretic II - Activision (Saw this for $2.99! And I loved this game!)
Shadow Man - Acclaim
Sid Meier's Gettysburg - Firaxis
Magic & Mayhem - Bethesda Softworks

3DO is giving it away

E-mail addresses that is. The 3DO company is givin' out free e-mail addresses with domains that are game-branded. For example: @mightandmagic.com or @armymen.com are but a couple. Just visit their web site to get yours @3DO.

It's simply marketing strategy to raise brand recognition and boost customer loyalty. But hell, it's free!
