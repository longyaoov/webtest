Cardamom Custard

Published Dec 08, 2014

  • Print

  • Like this recipe? Let us know!

  • Makes 8 servings

Ingredients

  • 1/2 cup crème fraiche

  • 2 cups whole milk

  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract

  • 4 cardamom pods, slightly crushed

  • 4 egg yolks, room temperature

  • 1 tablespoon cornstarch

  • 1/3 cup sugar

Instructions

In a large saucepan over medium heat, heat the crème fraiche, milk, vanilla extract and cardamom until hot. Without allowing it to boil, turn the heat to low and simmer for 15 minutes.

In a mixer with the paddle attachment, beat egg yolks, cornstarch and sugar until creamy.

With the mixer on medium, slowly pour the milk mixture into the egg mixture, ensuring the eggs don't get too hot and curdle. Once completely incorporated, pour the custard through a fine-meshed strainer from the mixing bowl back into the saucepan and place back on the stove over medium to low heat, stirring until the custard thickens, about 10-12 minutes.

Remove from heat and let cool, the custard will further thicken. Cover directly with plastic wrap to keep a skin from forming.

Serve dolloped over French toast.
