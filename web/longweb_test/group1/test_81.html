    Top 25 Smooth After Hour Spots

    Let's get one thing straight: we still love a traditional happy hour, but sometimes two-for one beers and a lame game of darts isn't going to cut it. When you're looking for something a lot more smooth, these 25 after work spots are great at taking happy hour to the next level, and break a few rules in the process (ping pong, city views, art installments!) Loosen your tie, put down the Blackberry, and get ready to wind down...

    Bar Delux

    Bar Delux is about as intimate as you can get in Hollywood. Skip the megaclub and get up close and personal with some friends in the plush 20s Deco rooms with cascading, cake-like chandeliers. If you're thirsty, and you will be, try a Ginger Black cocktail from the barman and live like a Gatsby.

    Buddakan

    This place practically invented Asian Fusion cuisine, but you can skip the happy Buddha thing and get a tranquility cocktail with some lemongrass-infused oolong tea. Tranquility Hour? Sure.

    Tavern on Rush

    Saddle up to the massive horseshoe bar for the hottest people watching in Chi-town from this tony tavern in the Gold Coast neighborhood. We recommend the special bloody mary with blue cheese-stuffed olives. Bangin.

    Coco Deville

    If you've got a sweet tooth, get yourself some signature cocktails, complimentary lolipops and loads of eye candy at this Hollywood hangout.

    Prime 112

    Steak. Martini. Repeat until not angry. Our just cut out the steak, it's still a great idea and they have fried Oreos if you just want dessert. Why not go to one of the best restaurants in the country for happy hour? The wait is shorter and then you have the whole night ahead of you. You heard it hear first.

    Green Door

    So they don't have a happy hour, per se, but that doesn't mean you can't make your own. The Green Door is done up in the style of a cozy, but lavish Victorian manor, except with the welcome contemporary upgrade of great DJs and a dance floor.

    Good Units

    Buried under the Hudson Hotel is a new venue for people who want to time travel to New York 15 years ago after work and get all party monster. In a good way! The modularly designed space also hosts exhibitions, instillations, fashion shows and film screenings (not to mention some epic after parties).

    Rose Bar & Jade Bar at the Gramercy Park Hotel

    Go before nine so you don't need a reservation, but if you stick around, your happy hour might just turn into a private concert with arena-caliber acts in a cafe-sized setting.

    Kiss & Fly

    Get a bottle of Ciroc and bring your dancing shoes, these euros take clubbing seriously. Be ready for some house and some open mouth. If you put 'kiss' in the name, it's on the agenda, people.

    Policy

    Live a little. Grab a booth and order 'on a whim' and let the bartenders play a little russian roulette with your cocktail. While you wait, you can take in the plush red and black interior that calls to mind devils and pinup girls.

    Hyde Lounge

    This club has been around a few years, but still holds a spot in the hearts of an advanced degree of party people. If you need to get it done, Hyde's cucumber melon margarita can do it.

    Roof at The Wit Hotel

    When you just need to rise above it all for that workday unwind, the roof at the Wit can take you there. Show-stopping views of the Chicago skyline and great cocktails like Jalisco Old Fashioned make a perfect mix, just make sure you're not afraid of heights...

    K Street Lounge

    A classic lounge in the white-wall and cocktails, bottle-service mold. If you're in DC and you're looking for the girls and some of the most bumping bass in town, this is the place.

    Gold Bar

    For all the gold and glitz (check out the wall of gold skulls), they manage to lose the pretense that some other downtown clubs carry. Toss in some amazing DJs like Jesse Marco and DJ Sinatra and Gold Bar may be the new gold standard in nightlife.

    Le Poisson Rouge

    A killer sound system and laid back atmosphere let you hang with the hippest for your downtown happy hour while you take in the best indie acts in town. And it's not just music and libation they serve up, this 'multimedia art cabaret' also features film, theater, dance and fine art.

    Blind Pig

    The cocktails AND the the jukebox are total classics here. If you want to get even more hands on, there's karaoke every Monday night. Still no? Well, if you'd rather leave it to the pros, there's live great indie, hip hop and experimental music too, just check the calendar.

    Winstons

    No pretense, no lines and for our money, one of the best places to kick back for a happy hour or four amidst killer 20s era Tinsel Town decor. Grab a leather banquet and just let go.

    The Burlington

    A relaxed corner bar with great cocktails on Chicago's western frontier. Grab a seat in one of the old church pews and have a couple stiff cocktails. You'll need them. The seating is notoriously uncomfortable, but still totally worth it.

    Red Maple

    Sometimes happy hours need a little extra spice. You know salsa spice, samba, merengue, you name it. Check the DJ before you visit to maximize your caliente. And if you're not in the mood for dancing, you can still get warm by the lounge's two roaring fire places.

    Santos Party House

    Santos is like a happy hour wild card where when someone plays it, you never know what you're going to get. From DJs to electro to some of the weirdest performances you've ever seen or heard, the one constant at Santos is that it rocks. Makes sense since it's co-owned by the cult rock hero, Andrew W.K.

    The Florida Room

    As far as we can tell, they named the state after the room, not the other way around. This swank bar is tucked away in the Delano but its no secret. Have a cocktail made by some of the best mixologists in the biz and pull up a seat next to the translucent piano to unwind. If you're lucky, you may get a chance to see part-owner Lenny Kravitz tickle the ivories.

    Centaur Bar

    This deco-tinged Detroit cathedral to cocktails is known for their martinis and from 4-7pm they're only five bucks. If you're in Detroit and you're part man, part horse and need a drink, this is clearly your spot.

    1Oak

    It's always better to know someone when you're going to the club with the most beautiful clientele in the city. But after a long day, if you want to drown in beautiful people and vodka, you've come to the right place. Please note: you'll need a sick day after this one.

    The Whistler

    When you need a cocktail--not just a mixed drink, but a real craft cocktail to ease your mind and take you to that happy place, we suggest The Whistler. Did we mention they're under $10? Not good enough? How about the fact that it's also one of the best indie music venues in Chicago? Thought that might help.

    Spin New York

    Like ping pong? Well, you do now. Order a Kazmanian Devil from the bar and then unleash your workday rage in a table tennis shootout in one of the most banging bars in New York right now.
