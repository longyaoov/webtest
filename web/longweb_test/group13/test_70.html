Tony Blair will promise to work with Bush and Merkel

Tony Blair is due to say that the world could be on the verge of a major breakthrough on long-term climate change goals.

The Prime Minister will tell the World Economic Forum that a “quantum shift” in the attitude of the US combined with the German presidency of the European Union presents an opportunity to agree the principles of a post-2012 agreement.

He will promise to work with President George Bush and the German Chancellor, Angela Merkel, towards a more “radical” and “comprehensive” successor to the Kyoto protocol during his final months in office.

In a keynote address to the conference in Davos, Switzerland, Mr Blair will say: “I believe we are potentially on the verge of a breakthrough.”

Mr Blair will praise Chancellor Merkel’s focus on climate change during her EU presidency and India and China’s engagement with the G8.

He will go on: “The mood in the US is in the process of a quantum shift.

“The president’s State of the Union address built on his ‘addicted to oil’ speech last year and set the first US targets for a reduction in petrol consumption.

“The German presidency gives us an opportunity to agree at least the principles of a new binding international agreement to come into effect when Kyoto expires in 2012 but one which is more radical than Kyoto and more comprehensive.

“More comprehensive because it includes America, China and India. It is a prize of tantalising significance and I think it is possible.”

Mr Blair is attending a private discussion on climate change in Davos on Saturday afternoon with US Senator John McCain. He will also have one-to-one talks with Mr McCain, the front-runner to be the Republican candidate in the next US presidential contest, before his speech later.
