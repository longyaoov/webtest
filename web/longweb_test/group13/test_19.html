The Future of Health event: SingularityU to host SA changemakers [Sponsored]

By Staff Reporter
Read nextYour move, Facebook: content search comes to Snapchat Stories

Are you interested in learning more about the Future of Health? Well, you’re in luck.

SingularityU’s Cape Town and Johannesburg Chapters will this week host the first events in its Future Of Series, focussing on the wellbeing of humankind.

SingularityU Cape Town and Johannesburg Chapters are a non-profit organisation, run by South African-based alumni, that strive to build a community in South Africa.

The Future of Health Cape Town event will see Discovery’s deputy CEO Dr Ryan Noach, synthetic biologist Dr Musa Mhlanga, and CEO of Next Biosciences Kim Hulett talk about the phenomenal power of exponential technologies in the health industry, and how these advancements will benefit humankind in the future.

The Johannesburg event will see Discovery’s CEO Dr Jonathan Broomberg take the place of Dr Ryan Noach, with Dr Musa Mhlanga, and Kim Hulett reprising their roles on the panel.

The Future of Health event will take place on Wednesday 5 April 2017, from 6.30pm to 8pm at the V&A Waterfront’s Workshop 17. A secondary event will be hosted in Johannesburg on Thursday 6 April 2017, from 6.30pm to 9pm at Hyde Park’s Park on 7th.

More information, including ticketing details, can be found below.

Future of Health, SingularityU Cape Town Chapter

Date: 5 April 2017
Time: 6.30 – 8pm
Location: Workshop 17, V&A Waterfront, Cape Town
Speakers: Dr Ryan Noach, Dr Musa Mhlanga, & Kim Hulett

Tickets can be purchased here.

Future of Health, SingularityU Johannesburg Chapter

Date: 6 April 2017
Time: 6.30pm – 9pm
Location: Park on 7th, Hyde Park, Johannesburg
Speakers: Dr Jonathan Broomberg, Dr Musa Mhlanga, & Kim Hulett

Tickets can be purchased here.
