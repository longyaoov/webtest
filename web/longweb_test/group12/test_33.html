Lakewood tower sells to Dallas investor - for the second time

Caddo Holdings bought the Lakewood Towers building on Gaston Avenue.

By Steve Brown

Real Estate Editor

Jun. 27, 2018
|
Updated 10:52 a.m. CDT
|
1 min. read

An East Dallas office high-rise has sold to a local investor.

It's the second time that Caddo Holdings has owned the 9-story Lakewood Towers complex. The property investor first bought the buildings in 2010 but later sold it.

Advertisement

"We owned and renovated Lakewood Towers previously and sold it in 2013," Justin Engler, Managing Director of Caddo Holdings, said in a statement. "We are pleased that Caddo as a company is once again a part of the Lakewood community. Principals within our company have a long history in the neighborhood."

The 9-story and 4-story buildings have more than 170,000 square feet of office space and are almost fully leased. Wells Fargo is a major tenant in the property.

The buildings were constructed in 1971 and 1981.

Advertisement

Commercial property firm CBRE brokered the building sale by an affiliate of Houston-based Highland Resources.

"Lakewood Towers' position as a fully renovated boutique office/medical property and its irreplaceable location is a highly sought-after combination for the tenant base that populates the surrounding high-end neighborhoods," CBRE's John Alvarado said in a statement.

Alvarado and CBRE's Gary Carr, Robert Hill, Eric Mackey, Evan Stone and Jared Chua negotiated the sale.
