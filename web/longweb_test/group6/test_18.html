Wannabe teen music star Chantelle Lee says she’ll give all her pop profit to charity

Singer whose fan includes Jay Z wants to donate royalties

  • LAUREN PROBERT
  • Published: 18:50, 14 Oct 2016
  • Updated: 22:15, 14 Oct 2016

    A BUDDING teen pop star has pledged to give her royalties to charity - and has boldly challenged the government to match her donation.

    Chantelle Lee, 16, from Whetstone, London has caught rapper Jay Z's attention with her unique brand of pop music.

    But rather than chasing fame and fortune, the teen wants to give all her profits from her single 'When I Think Of You’ to help children with learning difficulties.

    Chantelle is hoping her gesture will encourage the government to act and prioritise more money to help disabled children.

    The teen's pledge comes as a report shows 1.1 million pupils with special needs or disabilities are struggling to get the support they need in mainstream classrooms across England.

    The 16-year-old is tipped to be the UK's answer to Miley Cyrus' fictional singing alter ego Hannah Montana.
