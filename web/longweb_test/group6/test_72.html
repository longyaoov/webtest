    NW 136TH AVENUE STILL SEEN AS DETOUR TO REACH SAWGRASS

    Share this:

    • Click to share on Facebook (Opens in new window)
    • Click to share on X (Opens in new window)
    • Click to share on Bluesky (Opens in new window)
    By
    UPDATED: September 26, 2021 at 1:05 PM EDT

    State transportation officials have agreed to hurry the connection between the Sawgrass Expressway and Interstate 75, but a controversial detour will still have to be built by the Broward County Expressway Authority.

    Gerald Thompson, a county commissioner who chairs the expressway panel, said scheduling changes accepted by the district transportation office Thursday will shave at least nine months from the time the detour will have to be used.

    However, Northwest 136th Avenue will still have to serve as a connector between the Sawgrass and State Road 84 from May 1986 until about February 1987, said Thompson.

    Residents along the road, which will be widened to six lanes at a cost of $2.2 million, are asking the County Commission to help block the project.

    Under the plan to be presented to Florida Transportation Secretary Paul Pappas, one bridge connecting the Sawgrass and I-75 will be built ahead of schedule to cut the amount of time for using the detour.

    Thompson said that if the state won’t pay for necessary design work, the authority may pick up the tab.

    In addition, Thompson said the expressway panel may have to move to acquire right of way if it wants federal help in building a new interchange at I-95 and Southwest 10th Street in Deerfield Beach.

    He said he learned from state officials that the authority may need to demonstrate an ownership interest in the property before federal officials would agree to help pay for the new ramps, which could cost $20 million.

    The connection is proposed as a means of relieving traffic on I-95 when improvements to the crowded road begin next year.

    Thompson said the need to seek federal aid may slow the connection unless the traffic it would generate for the Sawgrass allows the authority to do the work itself.
