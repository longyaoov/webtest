    Kirkmeyer to keep Weld County Commissioner District 3 seat

    • Click to share on Reddit (Opens in new window)

    Kirkmeyer

    Expand
    By Katarina Velazquez | kvelazquez@greeleytribune.com
    UPDATED: May 13, 2020 at 6:12 AM MDT

    Incumbent Republican Barbara Kirkmeyer will keep her District 3 seat on the Board of Weld County Commissioners, per voters’ decisions Tuesday night.

    Kirkmeyer was ahead 53.4 percent of the vote, defeating her opponents, American Constitution Party Lynette Kilpatrick and Democrat Amanda Harper by a notable margin, according to results as of 10:30 p.m.

    Harper reeled in 33.3 percent of the vote, while Kilpatrick followed with 13.3 percent.

    “I think we’ve been doing a good job in the county and we’ve been going in the right direction,” Kirkmeyer said after her victory. “We have no debt, and I think people want us to keep going in the direction we’ve been going.”

    Kirkmeyer lives in a rural neighborhood near Erie. She ran for county commissioner the first time in the 1990s, served three terms, and then took a break. In 2008, she returned to county government and is serving her second term.

    She decided to run again for her second third term because she didn’t feel her job was finished quite yet. Her third term will give her four more years.

    “Transportation is a big issue for us,” Kirkmeyer said. “Obviously, we want to be fiscally conservative and keep with that. We want to make sure we’re a pro-business, strong economy bringing more jobs into the county.”

    Kirkmeyer said previously she wants to continue working on a handful of projects she’s taken on in the past eight years.

    U.S. 85 runs through much of Weld County, and about 40 miles of it sits in Union Pacific Railroad Co.’s land. The state’s lease expired years ago, and officials, including Kirkmeyer, are working on a new one.

    She also wants to help finish Weld’s largest public works project in history, turning Weld County Road 49 into a north-south highway from Kersey to Interstate 76 near Hudson. The project will be finished by the end of 2017 – something Kirkmeyer will see finish.

    Kirkmeyer also coordinates the human services department for the county, which administers social programs. Her plan is to work with another commissioner and train them in overseeing human services.

    “It will take four years to hand that off,” she said previously.
