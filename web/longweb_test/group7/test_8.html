    Six-word stories: IJ readers share short tales of luck

    IJ readers share short, short tales of luck

    Share this:

    • Click to share on Twitter (Opens in new window)
    • Click to share on Facebook (Opens in new window)
    • Click to print (Opens in new window)
    • Click to email a link to a friend (Opens in new window)
    By Vicki Larson | Marin Independent Journal
    PUBLISHED: March 25, 2019 at 12:00 PM PDT

    Next year will mark the 50th year since we celebrated the first Earth Day, founded by U.S. Sen. Gaylord Nelson on April 22, 1970, as a way to get us to be more conscious about our planet.

    And yet here we are, awash in plastic and waste from over-consumption, struggling to support alternative energy and still debating climate change as we experience increasingly hotter and extreme weather, rising sea levels and melting ice caps.

    How good are we doing at taking care of this fragile place we call home?

    For April, please share with the IJ a six-word story — just one — in honor of Earth Day, the natural world and our precious planet. Please make it a story with a beginning, middle and end, and not just six adjectives — make us laugh or cry or feel something. And please, we’ll be extremely grateful if you include your full name and your Marin hometown, write your short story in the body of the email not in the subject line or in an attachment), and use proper punctuation and check spelling.

    Send your six-word story to lifestyles@marinij.com by April 19 with “Six-Word Story” in the subject line. Then look for the stories in the April 30 lifestyles section and online.
