Colorado Author to Read Pug Poetry at Children’s Hospital Colorado for World Read Aloud Day

Posted by Mackenzie Spillane on February 1, 2019 in Adams County, Adams County Front, Arapahoe County, Arapahoe County Front, Arvada, Arvada Front, Aurora, Aurora Business, Aurora Education, Aurora Front, Aurora Opinion, Denver, Denver Business, Denver Education, Denver Front, Denver Opinion, Douglas County, Douglas County Front, Featured, Golden, Golden Front, Hot Topic Adams County, Jefferson County, Jefferson County Front, Lakewood, Lifestyles | 80 Views | Leave a response

On Feb. 1, schools, libraries, hospitals and even virtual meetup groups will enjoy listening to authors share their stories for World Read Aloud Day (WRAD). One Colorado Springs author, Jean Alfieri, will be reading aloud her most recent children’s poetry book, “Zuggy the Pug: Six Tales,” at Children’s Hospital Colorado on the Anschutz Medical Campus. The read-aloud gathering will host children and family members who choose to attend.

To celebrate this year’s WRAD, Alfieri will give attendees tote bags filled with stuffed-animal, Zuggy pugs, stickers and a copy of the book, “Zuggy the Pug: Six Tales.” Alfieri worked with Kathleen McBride, the director of the Association of Volunteers at Children’s Hospital Colorado, to set up the story time. “We’re excited to celebrate World Read Aloud Day and feature a local author whose stories bring laughter and smiles,” McBride said. “Hearing books read aloud is a great way to nurture the love for reading in children.”

In the past two years, since publishing her first books, Alfieri has visited and read at the children’s hospital two other times. “I love to hear the children’s giggles and see the looks on their faces as they hear about Zuggy swiping a donut off the counter or trying to shake some dog poo off of his paw,” she said. “You can see their eyes widen as they look at the illustrations and imagine everything happening.”

One of Alfieri’s primary goals with her books, and with the read-aloud events, is to ignite a love for reading and writing in the way that an author did for her at a very young age, she said. She remembers reading “Noisy Nora” by Rosemary Wells when she was 5 years old, and how the story sparked the love to read and write both poetry and short stories.

Having a similar goal of inspiration, LitWorld founded WRAD about 10 years ago to not only encourage reading and literacy, but also to highlight the right to literacy. “Literacy is not a gift given just to some lucky ones, it is a foundational human right that brings joy, economic independence, gender equity and a pathway out of poverty,” the website states. Pam Allyn, an author and educator, founded LitWorld in 2007 after a trip to Africa where she saw the impact of poverty limiting children who desired to read, write and share stories.

According to LitWorld, “World Read Aloud Day calls global attention to the importance of reading aloud, sharing stories, and the idea of literacy as a human right by bringing communities together across the world to read aloud and change the world.” For Alfieri, reading and sharing stories has always been a way to encourage young readers and to share important messages. Messages like the need and the benefit of adopting older, rescue dogs who desire love in their final years.

To join the celebration, you can take time on Friday to read aloud to a friend, family member or child; or, you can setup your own read-aloud event. Visit the LitWorld website for event recommendations and free activities. And, to find out more about Jean and her beloved pug, Zuggy, visit her website at jeanalfieri.com.
