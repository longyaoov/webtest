    UPDATED: August 15, 2018 at 1:18 PM PDT

    SAN JOSE — Cityview Plaza, a huge office, restaurant and retail complex in downtown San Jose, has been bought in a $284 million mega deal that highlights robust investor interest in the Bay Area’s largest city — and appears to mark the biggest commercial property sale in Silicon Valley so far in 2018.

    The deal comes amid a flurry of property sales and development proposals in nearby areas of downtown San Jose. The complex is a few blocks away from Diridon Station and the SAP Center, where Google plans a massive, transit-oriented complex of offices, restaurants, shops, residences, open spaces and other amenities in a development where 15,000 to 20,000 of the search giant’s employees would eventually work. It’s also across the street from the three-building Adobe Systems headquarters, which Adobe intends to expand with a fourth office tower.

    SJ Cityview, an affiliate of San Francisco-based developer Jay Paul, purchased the 580,000-square-foot complex, according to Santa Clara County property records that were filed July 23. The seller was Park Center Plaza Investors, an affiliate of realty investment firm Equus Capital Partners.

    The Jay Paul affiliate paid $283.5 million in cash for the property, which is bounded by West San Fernando Street, South Almaden Boulevard, Park Avenue and South Market Street, county documents show. The complex consists of 534,000 square feet of offices and 46,000 square feet of restaurant and retail space.

    “The mixed-use Class A campus consists of high-rise and mid-rise office buildings, retail and restaurant space, united by a newly designed plaza area with outdoor seating, artwork and water features,” according to an Equus web page about Cityview Plaza.

    Along with several large office buildings, Cityview Plaza contains 40,000 square feet of restaurant and retail spaces, including Specialty’s Cafe & Bakery, Morton’s Steakhouse, Scott’s Seafood, Peggy Sue’s, Bank of America, Heritage Bank and Wells Fargo Bank. In recent years, Equus launched a wide-ranging renovation that added a new fitness center, conference center, a fountain and upgraded landscaping, a property brochure stated.

    Colliers International and Cushman & Wakefield, commercial realty brokerages marketing the complex, stated in a brochure that Cityview Plaza commands a top-notch site.

    The property is perched near the Fairmont Hotel and the Paseo de San Antonio.

    “Cityview Plaza is centrally located in downtown San Jose” and features an “amenity-rich environment,” the brochure stated.

    The Cityview Plaza deal, at $283.5 million, looks to be the biggest commercial real estate acquisition so far during 2018 in Santa Clara County, as indicated by a review of major property purchases in the South Bay reported by this news organization.

    In January, a 64,000-square-foot Palo Alto office building occupied by Visa was bought for $138 million, notable for its head-spinning price of $2,165 a square foot. In March, a big office building in Mountain View that’s leased to Google, along with adjacent land suitable for development, was bought for $169.9 million.

    Steven Golubchik, Edmund Najera and David Hosler, executives with realty brokerage NKF Capital Markets, arranged the purchase of Cityview Plaza.

    “With one of the largest Caltrain stations and a BART line under development, downtown San Jose will be one of the most easily accessible and interconnected locations in Northern California, making this a strategic investment,” said Golubchik, a vice chairman with NKF Capital Markets.
