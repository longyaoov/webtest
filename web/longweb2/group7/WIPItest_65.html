Portland's Kim Hill leads USA women's volleyball into Rio Olympics quarterfinals

  • Updated: Aug. 15, 2016, 4:15 p.m.
  • |Published: Aug. 15, 2016, 3:15 p.m.
By
  • Oregonian/OregonLive Sports

The USA women's indoor volleyball team wrapped up Group B play at the Rio Olympics on Sunday with a 3-1 win over China (22-25, 25-17, 25-19, 25-19).

With the victory they finished undefeated in their group (5-0) and clinched a favorable quarterfinal matchup with Japan on Tuesday.

Team USA has been led by outside hitter Kim Hill, who tallied 54 points in five group play matches. Hill, who graduated from Portland Christian in 2008, has been playing for the national team since 2013.

USA women's volleyball has won the silver medal the past two Olympics, finishing behind Brazil both times. By going unbeaten in group play, they have set themselves up to potentially face Brazil for the gold medal on Saturday.

Here's the complete women's indoor volleyball schedule (all times Pacific):

Tuesday, Aug. 16

Quarterinals

South Korea vs. Netherlands, 5 a.m.

Japan vs. United States, 9 a.m.

Russia vs. Serbia, 1 p.m.

Brazil vs. China, 5:15 p.m.

Thursday, Aug. 18

Semifinals

Brazil-China winner vs. South Korea-Netherlands winner, 8 a.m.

Japan-United States winner vs. Russia-Serbia winner, 5:15 p.m.

Saturday, Aug. 20

Bronze Medal Match

Semifinal losers, 8 a.m.

Gold Medal Match

Semifinal winners, 5:15 p.m.
