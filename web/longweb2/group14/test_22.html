Bruce Springsteen’s Daughter Is Getting Her Own Gucci Riding Collection

By Josh Duboff
February 4, 2014

If you aren’t familiar with Jessica Springsteen, here’s what you need to know: she’s 22 years old; she’s a student at Duke University; she’s—in the words of Gucci creative director Frida Giannini—a “great horsewoman”; and, oh yeah, her dad is this up-and-coming musician named Bruce. Today, the luxury brand announced that Jessica will be the inspiration for a new riding collection, commemorating the four-year anniversary of Gucci’s decision to “return to the world of horse riding” in 2009. Jessica—who has been riding horses since she was four—will join the Gucci equestrian team, which already includes princess Charlotte Casiraghi, fifth in line to the Monaco throne.

The “core color” of the collection will be black, which seems appropriate, given the sartorial predilections of her father. (And we can only assume the Springsteen-inspired threads will appeal to your parents so intensely that they will practically shove the clothes down your throat, hoisting them in your direction on a near-daily basis, leading to your initial strong distaste of the collection . . . until, later in life, you find yourself coming to not only accept but love the songs—whoops, riding clothes! we mean riding clothes!—though you’ll never admit that reversal to them.)

If they’re looking for a Springsteen song to soundtrack an ad or whatever, we have to imagine “Born to Run” would be the likely choice (just imagine Jessica—sunset behind her—riding a horse in slow-motion as the chorus hits!), but we think they should just use the audio of Bruce’s excellent, well-reasoned response to Jimmy Fallon’s recent inquiry “Would you rather fight 100 duck-sized horses or one horse-sized duck?” for audio, synched to some B-roll of Jessica doing a few jumps.
