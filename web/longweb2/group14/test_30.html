    John McCain: Donald Trump and Hillary Clinton don’t know enough to handle terrorism or Middle East crisis

    Share this:

      Sen. John McCain and Sen. Lindsey Graham appear on CBS's "Face of the Nation" while in Kabul, Afghanistan on Sunday.

    Mary Altaffer and Chuck Burton/AP
    1 of 2

    McCain said neither Donald Trump nor Hillary Clinton have the chops to fight terrorism.

    Expand
    By Erin Durkin
    UPDATED: April 8, 2018 at 9:30 PM EDT

    Sen. John McCain said Sunday that neither Donald Trump nor Hillary Clinton has what it takes to take on terrorism and the crisis in the Middle East.

    “I don’t think either one of them have displayed what I think is the necessary strategy and outlook,” the Republican senator and former presidential nominee said on CBS’ “Face the Nation.”

    Speaking from Kabul, Afghanistan, alongside Sen. Lindsey Graham (R-S.C.), McCain said either candidate’s best hope would be to rely on military and diplomatic advisers like Gen. David Petraeus and Ryan Crocker “who know what we need to do to defeat this threat both militarily and diplomatically and other ways. And either one of them should call them in and do what they recommend.”

    McCain reiterated his tepid support of Trump, saying he had pledged to back his party’s nominee.

    “I have strong disagreements, and we’ve just been through several of them, and that’s my position,” he said.

    Graham, a former candidate for the GOP nomination, was more favorable to Clinton than Trump on foreign policy, calling Clinton’s plan for a no-fly zone over Syria “a great step in the right direction.”

    He panned Trump’s stance that it would be acceptable for Syrian President Bashar al-Assad to stay in office.

    “When Trump says it’s OK for Assad to stay, it tells me he has no idea what that means for the region,” he said. “The Syrian civil war is having an effect throughout the region. Mr. Trump, when you said Assad should stay, you need to rethink that.”

    The two GOP senators, part of a U.S. delegation to Afghanistan and other countries in the Middle East, warned President Obama against going forward with a plan to reduce U.S. troops from the current 9,800 to 5,500 by the end of this year, saying the country could again become a breeding ground for terrorist attacks against the United States.

    “The U.S. response should have been, a long time ago, not to withdraw everybody from Iraq,” McCain said. “And now the President’s on the verge of doing the same thing here in Afghanistan where things are not going well, and he’s insisting on cutting the number in half while the situation deteriorates. If we leave, and these forces take over, then there will be further attacks on the United States of America. By the way, there’s going to be further attacks on the United States of America as long as they have a base in Syria, but then they’ll have another base in Afghanistan,” he said.

    Graham also said that without a strong presence of American troops, “Afghanistan is going to become Iraq very quickly.”

    “If you forget about Afghanistan, you do so at your own peril,” he said.
