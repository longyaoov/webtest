Ford's Mystery Mini Is Version of Overseas Mazda Verisa: Spy Report

Published: Sep 30, 2009 8:00 PM EDT

At first, with our expert insiders refusing to ID this vehicle spotted near the company's HQ in Dearborn, Mich., we figured it for a future mini from Ford. And we had some advice for the Ford folks: Tighten up those body fits before you head into production, or this one won't even make its mark in the rental car market.

But now we've got a fix on the mystery mini: It's a cobbled version of the Mazda Verisa—a model sold overseas, not in the U.S. It is tiny at only 150 in. long, with a 1.6-liter, four-cylinder engine that should deliver fuel economy numbers in the high 30-mpg range.

So what's it doing at Ford's engineering center? We figure that Ford is looking at this vehicle as a model for its smallest sedan of the future, one that fits under the Focus in Ford's sedan lineup. Look for something similar—at least in size and chassis design—to show up at Ford dealer showrooms by 2009. —Jim Dunne
