A talented soprano who starred in several shows and won numerous awards has died aged 79.

Born in Hinckley on May 10 1934, Mary Bates was renowned for her singing voice which saw her win three first prizes in the 1959 Nuneaton Festival of Arts and also the Herbert Wise Challenge Cup at the prestigious Birmingham Music Festival in 1960.

A former member of the Leicester Operatic Society, Mary started singing at the age of 12 and went on to perform in shows including The Gipsy Baron, Carmen and Merry Widow at The Little Theatre in Leicester.

She lived in Hinckley and Burbage all her life up until two years ago when she moved to north Wales to live by the sea with close friend Richard Burbidge.

Having first met in 1988 Richard lived with Mary for 23 years after she moved in with his family. He said: “Everybody loved Mary, she was a very kind and very generous woman and she was well-known for her singing voice.

“She was a lovely lady.”

Mary lost her life on April 3 after an unexpected heart attack at her home in Criccieth.
