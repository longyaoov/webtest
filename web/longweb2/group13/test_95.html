Sharks acquire Reimer, Morin from Leafs for Stalock, Smith, 2018 pick

Posted on February 27, 2016 by Curtis Pashelka

The Sharks hope they solved their need for a backup goalie, acquiring goalie James Reimer and forward Jeremy Morin from the Toronto Maple Leafs on Saturday for Alex Stalock, forward Ben Smith and a conditional 2018 fourth round draft pick.

Finding a more experienced and reliable backup goalie figured to be Sharks general manager Doug Wilson’s wish list before Monday’s NHL trade line.

Reimer, 27, had an 11-12-7 record with Toronto this season with a .918 save percentage and a 2.49 goals against average. The 6-foot-2, 217-pound goalie is slated to become an unrestricted free agent this summer.

Morin has spent this season in the American Hockey League, playing 28 games with Rockford and 13 games with the Toronto Marlies.

“James is an experienced NHL goaltender who will help add to our depth at that position as we head down the stretch run of the season,” Wilson said in a statement. “Jeremy gives our group another versatile forward and a player that our coaching staff is familiar with. We’re excited to add them both to our organization.”

Getting only spot duty,  Stalock had a 3-5-2 record and a .884 save percentage in 13 games this season. He had played just once in the last 16 games.

Stalock, 28, had played in 62 games for the Sharks over five years. He had a breakout season in 2013-14, going 18-12-5 with a 1.87 goals against average as a backup to former No. 1 starter Antti Niemi. After the season, Stalock was given a two-year, $3.2 million contract.

But Stalock’s numbers dipped considerably last season, when he went 8-9-2 with a 2.62 goals against average. His last two starts this year have been overtime losses, and his last win was Jan. 12 when he stopped 21 of 22 shots in the Sharks’ 4-1 win over Winnipeg.

Smith was sent to the Barracuda on Jan. 22. Both he and Stalock will be unrestricted free agents this summer.

“Alex and Ben have both been important members of our team on the ice but even more importantly, they are both tremendous teammates and first-class individuals,” Wilson said. “They will be missed and we want to thank them for everything they have given to the San Jose Sharks organization and our fans.”

It is Wilson’s second deal with Leafs general manager Lou Lamoriello in under a week. On Monday, the Sharks traded for Nick Spaling and Roman Polak, giving up second round draft choices in 2017 and 2018 and Raffi Torres.

Smith is making $1.5 million this season and Stalock’s salary is $1.6 million. Reimer is making $2.0 million this season, as he’s in the final year of a two-year, $4.6 million deal.
