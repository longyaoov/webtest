Cumberland Valley's T.C. Warner, Cedar Cliff's Christopher Vassar earn silver medals at Class AAA states

  • Updated: Mar. 11, 2012, 3:47 a.m.
  • |Published: Mar. 11, 2012, 1:47 a.m.
By
  • Jim Carlson | Special to PennLive

There wasn't much doubt the 145-pound match was the most entertaining bout in the PIAA Class AAA finals Saturday night, and there was no doubt Chambersburg coach Doug Rine was going to agree with that notion.

View full sizeChambersburg's 145lb Garrett Hammon gets taken down by Cumberland Valley's TC Warner in the PIAA AAA wrestling championships Saturday, March 10, 2012. JOHN C. WHITEHEAD/The Patriot-News

"That's their fourth meeting this year and every one of them has been a clinic,'' Rine said about the back-and-forth rumble between his own Garett Hammond and T.C. Warner of Cumberland Valley.

"They're both accomplished and polished wrestlers and it's a thing of beauty to watch them wrestle.''

Rine can say that with a bit more ease because Hammond has won all four matches, and Saturday night's 12-9 victory earned him a coveted Class AAA gold medal after settling for silver last season.

But it was a beaut.

Warner, just a sophomore who already is 69-12 for his two-year career, took Hammond down in five seconds with a strong double-leg tackle. Then came a 12-point second period in which Hammond had eight of the 12, six of which off an escape and five-point hip toss.

"I went for an inside trip and a left-handed underhook and it got him a little bit off balance, enough that I tossed him,'' Hammond said. "I was able to wrestle off that lead. I'm real glad I got a state title, and I'll back at it next year.''

There's no doubt Warner will be, too. He beat the WPIAL champ, Heath Coles, 3-1, on Friday and knocked defending state champ Mitchell Minotti of Easton in Saturday morning's semifinals 4-3 in an ultimate tie-breaker bout.

The key to that win was Warner's ability to ride Minotti for three minutes, especially during the crucial overtime periods.

So he was feeling good about his easy takedown against Hammond, but not that good.

"I was pretty confident but I know he's kind of scrappy and he's gonna be dangerous in no matter what situation we're in,'' Warner said.

"I tried to turn him and I've never wrestled anyone who does such different stuff on bottom.''

But it was on their feet when Warner went bottom's up. "I think I'm better on my feet but I lost focus and he kind of hip-tossed me and caught me off guard,'' Warner said.

"He doesn't get that five points and it's a whole different story, but that's wrestling and I have to be focused all the time.

"But it was the chance of a lifetime,'' Warner said. "Not everybody can say they made the state finals. It was real fun out there and I wish the outcome was different, but I'm still pretty proud of my performance.''

VASSAR 2ND AT 138

Cedar Cliff junior Christopher Vassar also enjoyed a great run to the 138-pound finals but suffered the same fate as Warner against Steve Spearman of Erie McDowell.

Spearman, who won a state crown in 2010 over Central Dauphin's Tyler Buckman, was a bit too slick on his feet for Vassar and won 7-3.

The two took hand fighting to a new level as there was a lot of sparring and slapping from the neutral position. They didn't tie up much as Vassar wrestled neutral from his knees.

"I wanted to stay on his level so I wasn't coming up as high and he couldn't get that double-leg, but as you could see he took me down a couple of times,'' Vassar said.

"When we were on a knee I was staying inside a little bit and I didn't take a shot but was staying back.

"I just need to get back in the (wrestling) room and get better,'' Vassar said.

With a career slate of 106-24, Vassar's already pretty good and he was "thrilled" with his spot in the finals.

"I want to get to the top of that podium so I have to get back in the room for next year,'' he said.

NOTES

Canon-McMillan won the team title with 102 points to Central Dauphin's 67. ... Chance Marsteller of Kennard-Dale won the Outstanding Wrestler Award. ... District 3 had 11 wrestlers in the finals and five won gold medals.
