Tropics: Maria, Jose keep churning in Atlantic

J.D. Gallop
Florida Today

As expected, Tropical Storm Maria strengthened into a hurricane, heading along a similar path as Hurricane Irma and taking a menacing aim for the already storm-ravaged Lesser Antilles and Puerto Rico, according to an 11 p.m. Sunday National Hurricane Center advisory.

The question of whether it poses a threat to east coast of Florida remains up in the air as weather forecasters continue to monitor computer models.

“It’s really too far to say anything about any impact here,” said Jessie Smith, a meteorologist with the National Weather Service in Melbourne.

For now, Maria is a Category 1 hurricane with sustained winds of 85 mph and is moving west-northwest at 13 mph.

Hurricane warnings and watches have been issued for several islands, including Antigua, Barbuda – which was left devastated by Irma – Montserrat and others. Weather forecasters are also warning residents in Puerto Rico, the British and U.S. Virgin Islands to monitor the storm as it continues to intensify, possibly as a Category 3 hurricane as it hits the northeast Caribbean.

The hurricane is expected to bring high winds and torrential rains in some areas, possibly causing mudslides and posing a danger to those still struggling to recover from Hurricane Irma’s deadly passage. Authorities believe Irma is responsible for the deaths of at  least 68 people from the Caribbean to Florida.

Maria is also one of three storms being monitored by the National Hurricane Center.

Tropical Depression Lee – with sustained winds at 35 mph - continued to weaken overnight and could possibly below a remnant low pressure system by Tuesday, officials said.

It does not pose a threat to any surrounding land.

It's already downgraded and a fish storm, stays out to sea. Hurricane Jose – a Category 1 hurricane, continues to move north along the eastern shoreline of the U.S.

Smith said having multiple storms at this time of year is not too unusual.

“This is the peak of the hurricane season,” Smith said. “It’s what we expect.”

Hurricane Jose

Hurricane Jose is projected to stay offshore, but has been creating high surf and dangerous rip currents along Space and Treasure coast beaches for several days.

 

At 11 p.m. Sunday, Jose was maintaining its intensity as it moves northward  at 9 mph. Jose had maximum sustained winds of 90 mph. It could cause some direct impacts from Delaware northward to New England. 

Tropical Depression Lee

Tropical Depression Lee was briefly a tropical storm before being downgraded Sunday morning. At 11 p.m. Sunday, this storm, which is expected to be a remnant low Monday, had maximum sustained winds of 35 miles per hour and was moving west-northwest at 10 mph.

 

Hurricane Maria

Hurricane Maria is projected to approach the Lesser Antilles. Sunday evening, Maria was a Category 1 hurricane with sustained winds of 85 mph and was moving west-northwest at 13 mph.

It was expected to become a major hurricane as it moves near the Leeward Islands.

 

A hurricane watch is in effect for Antigua, Barbuda, St. Kitts, Nevis, and Montserrat, Saba and St. Eustatius, St. Maarten, St. Martin and St. Barthelemy, and Anguilla.

A hurricane warning is in effect for Guadeloupe and Dominica.

A tropical storm watch is in effect for Martinique, Barbados, and St. Vincent and the Grenadines.

A tropical storm warning is in effect for St. Lucia.
