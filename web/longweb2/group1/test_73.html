No. 10 Glencoe girls edge Southside 48-47 in 3rd meeting

Kevin Taylor Times Staff Writer

GLENCOE — Class 3A, No. 10 Glencoe erased a six-point Southside lead by the 5:30 mark of the fourth period and then swapped leads with the Panthers three times in the period to hold on for a 48-47 victory in girls high school basketball Thursday.

This marked the second time the Yellow Jackets girls basketball team has defeated Southside in one season, which is a first in Glencoe's basketball history, and the victory gave the Jackets the win in the rubber match this season between the teams; Southside won the meeting in December.

The game also served as a preview of what may be to come for both squads since area tournament play begins next week.

Yellow Jackets coach Daniel Cook said Thursday's game had a playoff feel.

"First off, Southside is a really, really good team. When it comes to regional games, you aren't going to play a bad team. So you have to find little cracks here and there to get a point here and there. That's how this game felt tonight," Cook said.

The Panthers (17-5) had a five-point lead with 2:38 left to play and looked to play keep away from the Yellow Jackets.

But that decision soon led to a tie game with 1:07 left.

Southside reclaimed its lead with 42 seconds left when Macie Williams had a chance to convert a three-point play. That ended up being the last bucket of the night for the Panthers. Williams finished the night with 12 points. She scored 10 of her points in the second half.

Glencoe drew to within a point with 36 seconds left when Abby Vice converted one of two free throws. Then Lauren Cole seemingly snatched victory out of the hands of Southside when she muscled in a basket with 20 seconds to go with a chance to convert a three-point play.

Cole's free-throw attempt was just shy, and Southside was given a chance to win the game. Williams was fouled with 18.6 seconds left but both of her free-throw attempts were off target and the Yellow Jackets managed to eke out the victory.

The win also came almost two weeks to the day after Glencoe beat the Panthers for the Etowah County title.

"It's incredible," Cole said of the win over Southside. "We didn't play as good as we did in the county tournament, but we still came out with a win, so I'm happy."

Cole finished with eight points. Anna Beth Giles and Abby Vice led the Yellow Jackets (17-8) with 13 points each.

Alexis Thompson led the Panthers with a game-high 20 points.

Glencoe closes out the regular season Friday when it plays host to Pleasant Valley. Southside is off until Wednesday when it plays either Etowah or Alexandria for the area tournament championship.

In the boys game, the Panthers (9-17) defeated the Yellow Jackets 49-22.

Blake Habyan paced Southside with 20 points. Brock Habyan finished with 12 points.

Cam Harris had six points, tops for Glencoe.
